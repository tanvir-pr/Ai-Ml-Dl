# Week 5: Classification algorithms

**Month 2 · Classical machine learning**

## Purpose
Train and compare four major families of **supervised classifiers** on tabular data. This week connects Week 3 probability (Bayes) and Week 4 optimization (logistic loss) to practical models in scikit-learn.

## Topics covered (in order)

| # | Notebook | Focus |
|---|----------|--------|
| 1 | `01_logistic_regression.ipynb` | Sigmoid, log-odds, linear boundary, `predict_proba`, multiclass |
| 2 | `02_knn_algorithm.ipynb` | Distance metrics, choice of *k*, cross-validated *k*, scaling |
| 3 | `03_naive_bayes.ipynb` | Gaussian vs multinomial NB, independence assumption, text-friendly variants |
| 4 | `04_svm.ipynb` | Max margin, kernels (linear, RBF), `C` and `gamma`, scaling |

## Learning outcomes
- Explain when **logistic regression** is appropriate vs **KNN** vs **Naive Bayes** vs **SVM**.
- Plot or reason about **decision boundaries** for linear and kernel models.
- State why **feature scaling** matters for KNN, SVM, and (often) logistic regression.
- Describe **OvR** vs **multinomial** multiclass for logistic regression.
